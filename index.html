// 01 — Structure

The Thermal Skin
Wall System

The walls are not walls — they are a three-layer climate system, built like the Earth's own atmosphere. Each layer has a specific job. Together they regulate temperature passively, before the intelligent systems even need to activate.

Outer
Shield
Outer Layer — The Shield

Reflective ceramic or light metal coating. Bounces away solar heat in summer. Protects against wind and rain year round. First line of defence against the outside world.

Thermal
Mass
Middle Layer — The Store

Stone, concrete, or phase-change materials. Absorbs heat slowly during the day and releases it slowly at night. Acts as a natural battery for warmth — charging when the sun shines, discharging when it doesn't.

Comfort
Layer
Inner Layer — The Comfort Zone

Insulated panels that keep the interior temperature stable regardless of what the outer layers are doing. The buffer between the active climate management and the living space.

// 02 — Earth Coupling

The Earth
as Climate Control

Underground Temperature Regulation

Pipes and channels run beneath the house through the ground. The Earth maintains a stable temperature at depth regardless of the season above. In the UK that means roughly 10–15°C year round.

In summer, hot air passes through the underground pipes, cools to ground temperature, and returns inside refreshed. In winter, cold incoming air passes through the same pipes, warms to ground temperature, and enters the home already heated. The Earth does the work for free.

// 03 — Core Systems

Four Systems
Working as One

🌬️
Airflow System

Hidden vent channels run through walls and floors. In hot weather they pull cool air in from the ground-level shaded side and push hot air out through roof vents. In cold weather they trap warm air inside and circulate it evenly through every room.

All vents open and close automatically based on sensor readings — no manual adjustment needed.

🏠
Roof System

A dual-function roof with two active layers. The solar layer generates electricity continuously. The thermal capture layer absorbs heat when the home needs it in winter and switches to a reflective mode in summer to bounce excess heat away.

The roof is both power station and climate shield depending on what the home needs at any given moment.

🪟
Smart Windows

Adaptive glass that darkens automatically in strong sunlight to prevent overheating, and clears in low light to maximise warmth and natural light. Reduces solar heat gain in summer while retaining warmth in winter — without ever blocking the view.

🧠
Climate Core AI

The brain of the system. Sensors throughout the home read inside temperature, outside temperature, sun intensity and humidity continuously. The Climate Core uses that data to manage airflow vents, heat storage, solar routing and temperature zones in real time.

The home adapts before you feel it change.

// 04 — Automatic Modes

How It Adapts
Automatically

🔥 Hot Day
☀️ Outer shell switches to reflective mode — sunlight bounced away
🌍 Underground pipes pull cool ground-temperature air into the home
💨 Roof vents open — hot air rises and escapes naturally
🪟 Windows tint automatically to block solar heat gain
🌬️ Floor vents open on shaded side — cool air drawn in at ground level
❄️ Cold Day
☀️ Outer shell switches to absorbing mode — solar heat drawn in
🧱 Thermal mass walls store heat during daylight hours
🌙 Walls release stored heat slowly through the night
🌬️ Vents close and circulate warm air evenly through every room
🌍 Incoming cold air warmed by underground pipes before entering
